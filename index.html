Excerpts from the book

Inane Campus rules:

Stepping on lawns is strictly prohibited. You might get shot at sight, or worse, be exposed to two and a half hours of Gunda. There are exceptions of course; like if it's your convo, or poly results are being announced..

Conversing/Looking at/Walking with the females in front of KB or SB is banned.

Sleeping in the library is prohibited. You’d think it is obvious, but wait till you open textbooks in library. The induced sleep can knock you out in seconds. On the other hand, watching TV Shows with your paramour and PDA in the journal section is not.

The girls are locked in their Bhawans after 10pm. Yes, locked in! And all notices referring to them as ‘inmates’ don't help much either.

Under no circumstances should you send cheeky messages or poke the better looking females of the institute on online social media without knowing them personally. It might result in you getting reported or blocked or both.

Asking a fellow fresher his JEE rank is fine, as long as you don’t tell yours in response.

#The guide has everything

#history
The institute has its roots way back in 1845. It was the time when speaking in a british accent did not make you a homo. It was also the time when people dressed up in 3-piece suits just to go have dinner in the Bhawan messes (where they were served in silverware by turbaned waiters)!

#tradition
Although everyone signs the undertaking form which promises that they won’t be going anywhere near the canal or aquedcut, there’s hardly any undergrad who hasn’t visited it once.

#bakkar
The most path breaking research of course happens in the Civil department. This is because most of their research tends to involve breaking down existing roads and laying them again, in the hope of finding that elusive X to Y ratio, where X and Y are two materials no one else in the institute has any idea about.

#information
By now you must be aware of the places called Prakash, Sagar and Olive.But what about the place that’s even open at 3am in the morning and perhaps serves the best maggi in the whole town of Roorkee?

the place which is also known as the Mona-lisa of chicken shacks in Roorkee?

definitions

Chapo:
Traditionally meaning chai-pakoda, this term has been now reduced to mean party or treat, or in the worst case, as a euphemism for ‘I like you, but I don’t really have any topic to talk with you. Can we meet?’
“Hey Meenakshi! What about your birthday chapo?”
“My birthday was last month. Jerk.”

Tut
Short for tutorial. Refers to an assignment professors give in the (unrealistic) hope of students completing it by themselves and submitting. Have some weightage in your final CG, so don’t risk even trying to solve it yourself.
“This Maths tut is like a girl’s attention. I just don’t get it.“

Bakchod
A term used to describe someone who frequently indulges in Bakchodi.
or
A worthless person or thing.
or
Any course offered by the Humanities department.
“Rajeev bakchod banda hai yaar. He still believes RG should’ve been the PM of this country.”
